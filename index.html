My Work and Projects

My Work

Caltech SURF Research Fellow, University of Washington (July 2022-present)

Using Explainable AI to simplify model interpretations of the relationship between gene expression and neuropathologies related to Alzheimer's Disease.

This was my first large step into the world of Machine Learning research. I was surrounded by graduate students-- which made me want to learn as much as humanly possible in the short time frame I had as a SURF student. It was both overwhelming and inspiring at the same time. I loved it. (and it's not over yet!)

I want to thank Professor Su-In Lee and Nicasia Beebe-Wang for being incredible mentors. I also want to thank William H. and Helen Lang for supporting my research this Summer.

Software Engineering Intern, SkyCool Systems (April 2022-July 2022)

As a Software Engineering Intern at SkyCool Systems, I assisted with data collection, standardized the data pull pipeline across several sites, and wrote modules to facilitate expansion to further sites.

I am passionate about writing solid, versatile, and reusable solutions to real-world problems-- that's what I got to do here. I got the chance to take several systems and unify them under a generalized codebase. It was wonderfully fun in a way that both coursework and research couldn't be! I am beyond grateful to Eli Goldstein, Teresa Peters, Todd Krajewski, and the rest of the SkyCool team for being wonderful mentors to me.

SEAP Student Intern, U.S. Naval Research Laboratory (September 2020-August 2021)

Worked under Dr. Jason Kooi to analyze and model the effects of coronal Faraday rotation (FR) on radiation from extragalactic radio sources in order to learn more about the solar magnetic field.
